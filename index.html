Intro to MPC

Secure Multi-party Computation (MPC) allows multiple parties to jointly compute a result over their private inputs without anyone revealing their individual data to the others. In this tutorial, you will go through the protocol yourself before watching a full demonstration.

The Scenario

Maria is getting married in a week and just got a new hairstyle. She asks her friends Alice, Bob, and Lisa to rate it honestly from 1 to 5. She needs honest opinions, but she is scared that if each friend tells her their opinion directly, they will overestimate their score because of friendship pressure. At the same time, she does not want her friends to discuss her appearance and share their scores with each other. She applies the MPC protocol to reveal the average score for her hairstyle so that no individual knows the scores of others.

Step 2 — Split each score into 3 random fragments

Each score is split into three values that sum to the actual score. The additional numbers create noise — values that do not reveal the actual score on their own, but can be used to compute the final result.

Spin a randomizing wheel that picks a noise number from -1000 to 1000. Each spin results in a random number. Then, calculate the third number by subtracting those random numbers from the actual score.
